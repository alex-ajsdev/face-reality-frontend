About Face Reality

Unraveling the Art of AI-Generated Faces

In the rapidly evolving world of artificial intelligence, the ability of algorithms to generate human-like faces has reached unprecedented levels of realism. Face Reality is a project created to explore the prowess of different AI algorithms, gauging their proficiency in crafting lifelike human faces. The other half of the project puts us humans, to the test. AI image generation will only improve over time. Therefore, discerning AI-generated images from real ones is a skill of utmost importance. How well can you spot the difference?

The AI Contenders

Our platform evaluates some of the most advanced AI algorithms in the realm of GAN image generation, including:

  • StyleGAN: Known for its intricate style-based generators.
  • StyleGAN2: An enhancement of StyleGAN, rectifying some of its predecessor's flaws.
  • ProGAN: Progressive Growing of GANs, a method that scales up from low-resolution images, adding layers that deal with increasing detail.

The Human Challenge

While AI continues to advance, the human eye remains a sophisticated tool, often able to catch nuances and imperfections that machines overlook. Through this project, I invite users to challenge themselves, testing their ability to differentiate between AI-generated and real human faces.

Tips to Identify AI-Generated Faces

While AI has made significant strides, there are still some telltale signs that can give away an AI-generated face:

  1. Asymmetry: Real human faces are rarely perfectly symmetrical. AI-generated faces might exhibit perfect symmetry, especially in features like eyes or ears.
  2. Background Anomalies: Often, AI focuses on the face, leading to overlooked or distorted backgrounds.
  3. Hair Strands: AI can sometimes struggle with the intricate details of hair, leading to unnatural clumps or strands.
  4. Eyes & Reflections: The reflections in the eyes might be mismatched or may depict unrelated items.
  5. Teeth & Mouth: Look for overly consistent teeth or odd interactions between lips and teeth.
  6. Artefacts & Noise: Tiny imperfections, unusual patterns, or colour inconsistencies can be a giveaway.
